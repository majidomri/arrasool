What is the Quran?

The Quran is the final guidance from the Lord of all worlds. Revealed to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) through angel Gabriel, confirming all previous divine books. This is the Book about which there is no doubt, a guidance for those conscious of One True God (Allah). It is for those who believe in the unseen, establish prayer, and spend from what Allah has provided. Believers accept what has been revealed to Muhammad and previous prophets and have certainty in the Hereafter. They are based upon right guidance and are successful. For those who disbelieve, whether warned or not, they will not believe. Allah has set a seal upon their hearts, hearing, and vision, and for them is a great punishment.

What is the subject of the Quran?

The subject of the Quran is Humans. It explains what leads to human success and well-being and what does not. It discusses the reality of the universe, its beginning, end, and system. It tells who the Creator, Owner, Organizer, and Regulator of the universe is, what His attributes are, and the reality upon which this entire system is biosed. It explains the status and position of humans in this world and the correct path of thought and action for them. This book provides a comprehensive code of life for every aspect of human existence. It explains the consequences of following the right path and the results of treading the wrong paths in this world and the hereafter. It tells that this world is temporary, a test for humans and jinn (devil), and the real place is the hereafter where the righteous will receive Paradise and its eternal blessings, with a life that has no end. It also states that Hell is for the wretched, where they will dwell forever, never dying, and it is a very bad abode.

What is its example?

The relationship of this book to humans is like that of students in an exam hall with their question papers; human actions are the answer sheet, recorded and rewarded on the Day of Judgment with utmost fairness. Alternatively, imagine a boat lost in the sea without contact, where systems have failed and provisions are exhausted, with no shore in sight and surrounded by the threat of death. In their desperation, the sight of a bird brings immense relief and hope, prompting them to follow it, hoping it leads to safety. Similarly, the Quran relates to humans when they find their life's resources depleted and the world, despite its vastness, becomes constricting. Amidst tyranny and chaos, as humanity faces its end, the Quran stands as the protector of life, wealth, honor, and lineage, instilling peace and tranquility. The Quran guides to a steadfast path, free from deviations and fluctuations. It speaks of people until the Day of Judgment, including you. Have you sought to discover what it mentions about you? If one ignores or denies the Quran and discourages others, who could be more unjust?
This is the only book in the world that has deeply and universally impacted human thoughts, morals, civilization, and lifestyle to an unparalleled extent. Its influence first changed a nation, and then that nation sparked a revolution in minds from Spain to Malaysia.
In light of these truths, the Quran in the second verse of the second Surah of the first part says about this book, "This is the Book about which there is no doubt." This verse aims to make humans understand that while all other books discussing metaphysics and truths beyond perception are based on conjecture, this is a book entirely based on knowledge and truth. Since its author is the knower of all truths, there is absolutely no room for doubt in it!
